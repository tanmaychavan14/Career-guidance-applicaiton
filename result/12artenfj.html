ENFJ (Protagonist)

ENFJ (Protagonist) is a personality type with the Extraverted, Intuitive, Feeling, and Judging traits. These warm, forthright types love helping others, and they tend to have strong ideas and values. They back their perspective with the creative energy to achieve their goals. People with the ENFJ personality type (Protagonists) feel called to serve a greater purpose in life. Thoughtful and idealistic. ENFJs are born leaders, which explains why these personalities can be found among many notable politicians, coaches, and teachers. Their passion and charisma allow them to inspire others not just in their careers but in every arena of their lives, including their relationships. Few things bring people with the ENFJ personality type a deeper sense of joy and fulfillment than guiding friends and loved ones to grow into their best selves.


ENFJ Strengths

• Receptive
• Reliable
• Passionate
• Altruistic
• Charismatic


ENFJ Weaknesses

• Overly Idealistic
• Condescending
• Intense
• Overly Empathetic

career Stream career cluster description
Arts Art Critic/Journalist ENFJs may pursue careers as art critics or journalists, where they can analyze and critique artistic works, trends, and movements. They can write articles, reviews, or commentary for publications, websites, or media outlets, contributing to cultural discourse and shaping public opinion about art.
Event Planner/Coordinator ENFJs can leverage their organizational skills and creativity in roles as event planners or coordinators, organizing and executing various corporate events such as conferences, meetings, or team-building activities. They can handle logistics, coordinate vendors, and ensure memorable experiences for attendees.
Arts Educator ENFJs may find fulfillment in roles as arts educators, teaching and inspiring students of all ages in schools, community centers, or arts organizations. They can create engaging lesson plans, foster creativity, and encourage self-expression through various art forms such as visual arts, music, or theater.
Art Entrepreneur ENfj with an entrepreneurial spirit may thrive as art entrepreneurs, where they can start and manage their own creative businesses. They can explore opportunities in areas such as art galleries, design studios, event production, or creative consulting, leveraging their creativity and business acumen to achieve success.
